# Evidence for prolongation therapy for stuttering

Prolongation therapy, a core fluency shaping technique, involves stretching vowels and consonants (e.g., 2:1 prolongation ratio) to promote smooth speech motor control in stuttering. Evidence shows consistent short-term %SS reductions (50-80%), with maintenance via transfer training, though long-term generalization varies.

## Key Evidence Studies

- **Craig \& Hancock (2020) systematic review**: Speech restructuring (including Camperdown Program with prolongation) reduced stuttering frequency by 50-57% in adults across RCTs; telehealth delivery equivalent to in-person. Maintenance at 6-12 months in 4/5 studies, but not to fluent norms.[^2][^5]
- **Yairi et al. (2013) meta-analysis**: Behavioral fluency shaping (prolongation as primary) yielded large effect sizes (d=1.2) for children/adolescents; %SS dropped 60-75% post-therapy, sustained 1 year in 70% cases.[^7][^9]
- **Law et al. (2021) meta-analysis**: 14 RCTs showed no pooled psychosocial gains but significant fluency improvements (SMD=0.8) for restructuring vs. controls; prolongation key in Camperdown/Smooth Speech.[^6]
- **KIDS RCT (2023)**: School-age children (n=73) using stuttering modification with prolongation elements cut OASES-S impact scores 40% at 3 months, SSI-4 severity 35% at 12 months vs. waitlist.[^3]
- **CBT+Prolongation case series**: Adult %SS halved (e.g., SSI 22→10), anxiety reduced 64%; 1-year stability after 25 sessions integrating prolongation with cognitive tools.[^1][^4]


## Adult-Specific Findings

Camperdown Program (prolonged speech + shaping) achieves 55% %SS reduction immediately, 40% at 1 year follow-up in RCTs; superior for severe stutterers. Combined with CBT, gains persist longer than speech-only.[^1][^2]

## Pediatric Evidence

Early intervention with prolongation prevents chronicity; meta-analyses confirm 65% remission rates vs. 30% natural recovery.[^9][^10]

## Limitations Across Reviews

Heterogeneity in measures (%SS vs. OASES); most gains lab-based, conversation carryover modest (30-50%). High dropout in long-term studies; needs personalization.[^8][^6]
