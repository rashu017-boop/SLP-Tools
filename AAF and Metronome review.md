## Evidence for DAF, FAF, MAF, and Metronome Pacing

**Altered auditory feedback (AAF) therapies including DAF, FAF, MAF, and metronome pacing demonstrate robust immediate fluency benefits for stuttering, with evidence varying by method and setting.** **Reviews below synthesize key studies, focusing on % syllables stuttered (%SS) reductions, tasks (reading, monologue, conversation), and carryover effects.**[^1][^2]

## DAF Review

Delayed Auditory Feedback (DAF, 40-200 ms delay) reduces %SS by 70% immediately in reading/monologue; optimal ~100 ms. Long-term: 3 months home use (30 min/day) yielded 55% carryover without therapy; 14-hour mediated learning + DAF cut %SS from 9% to 4.1% (55% reduction, 1-year stable). RCTs: DAF+tDCS enhanced fluency over sham (p<0.05); systematic reviews note diversity but benefits for severe stutterers.[ from Andrade 2011] Commercial devices (e.g., SpeechEasy, Pocket Speech Lab) show 44-80% reductions in calls/monologues, stronger for severe cases.[^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^1]

## FAF Review

Frequency Altered Feedback (FAF, ±0.5-1 octave shift) yields 50-74% %SS reductions across tasks/audience sizes; half-octave downshift often optimal. Combined DAF+FAF (75 ms/0.5 octave): 62% reading, 34% dialog reductions; superior to solo FAF (31-64%). Immediate effects: 64% in females, 31% males; effective in monologues (44%) but habituates faster than DAF. Reviews confirm fluency without rate slowing; benefits blocks > repetitions/prolongations.[^6][^8][^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^1]

## MAF Review

Masked Auditory Feedback (white noise masking self-hearing) cuts stuttering-like disfluencies (SLDs) 23-45%; amplified feedback (AAF) 27-67%. Severe groups: All AAF types (DAF/MAF/AAF) significant vs. baseline (p<0.05, SLDs drop 45-67%); moderate: MAF/AAF only (p=0.042). No rate impairment; interjections/hesitations unchanged. Sparse RCTs but consistent across spontaneous speech; severe > moderate response.[-28 from Lincoln review][^2][^14][^15]

## Metronome Review

Metronome-paced speech normalizes kinematics (%SS ~0% paced), reduces variability in vocal tract (alveolar/palatal/velar) to fluent levels. fMRI: 8-week training alters basal ganglia; 15 min/day yields fluency/naturalness gains. Rate-flexible pacing maintains benefits; superior to baseline in reading/monologue/dialogue. Systematic reviews include it in adult interventions; robust for timing deficits but conversation generalization pending.[^13][^16][^17][^18][^19][^20][^21][^22]
