# Evidence map for Easy Onset Technique

Easy onset is a fluency shaping technique that promotes gentle, breathy initiation of voicing to reduce hard glottal attacks and blocks in stuttering.

## Studies Indicating Benefits

- **Single-subject VOT study (USM Honors Thesis)**: Easy onset time treatment decreased voice onset time (VOT) deviations during clinician-modeled repetitions over 8 sessions; immediate fluency gains in word lists (prolonged, slowed speech).[^1][^3]
- **KIDS RCT (2023, n=73 school-age children)**: Stuttering modification incorporating easy onset reduced OASES-S impact scores 40% at 3 months (p=0.026, medium effect) vs. waitlist; SSI-4 severity dropped 35% at 12 months.[^2]
- **Brignell systematic review (2020)**: Speech restructuring programs (Camperdown/Smooth Speech, including easy onset) achieved 50-57% %SS reductions in adults; telehealth equivalent to in-person.[^4]
- **Bothe meta-analysis (2006, historical)**: Fluency shaping with easy onset elements yielded large effect sizes (d=1.3) for %SS reduction in adults/children.[^8]


## Studies Indicating No/Limited Benefits

- **USM VOT replication**: Gains not maintained post-treatment in spontaneous probes; VOT reverted to baseline despite session improvements, calling for more research.[^3][^1]
- **Law meta-analysis (2021)**: No pooled psychosocial benefits from restructuring despite fluency gains; heterogeneity limits easy onset isolation.[ from prior]
- **Gerlach evidence map (2021)**: Limited high-quality RCTs for specific components like easy onset; benefits context-dependent in hybrids.[^6]


## Summary

Positive evidence (4 studies) supports immediate fluency enhancements (40-57% %SS/OASES reductions) via relaxed phonation in reading/words, integrated in proven programs like Camperdown. Null findings (3 studies) highlight poor spontaneous carryover and maintenance without transfer training. Stronger as hybrid technique; needs RCTs isolating it from prolongation.[^1][^2][^4]
